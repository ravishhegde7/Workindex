Frequently Asked Questions — ITR Filing Hubli

I'm a trader in Hubli — which ITR form do I need? ▾
Most small traders in Hubli use ITR-4 under presumptive taxation scheme (Section 44AD) which simplifies compliance significantly. Larger businesses with turnover above ₹2 crore or those requiring audit use ITR-3. Your CA will determine the right form based on your turnover and nature of business.

What is presumptive taxation and is it right for my Hubli business? ▾
Presumptive taxation (Section 44AD) allows small businesses with turnover below ₹2 crore to declare 8% (6% for digital receipts) of turnover as profit without maintaining detailed books. It significantly reduces compliance burden for traders. A Hubli CA on WorkIndex will advise whether it's beneficial for your situation.
I have both agricultural income and business income — how is my ITR filed? ▾
Agricultural income is exempt from income tax but is included for rate purposes when you have other taxable income. The filing becomes slightly more complex. CAs in Hubli-Dharwad are familiar with this combination since it's common in North Karnataka.
What is the ITR filing deadline? ▾
31 July for individuals and non-audit businesses. 31 October for businesses requiring statutory audit. Belated returns by 31 December with a late fee. For businesses in Hubli, it's advisable to start the process by June to avoid last-minute document collection issues.
